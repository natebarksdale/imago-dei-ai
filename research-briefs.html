Are We Merely Machines?
Keynote: Prof. Rosalind Picard ‚Ä¢ Friday 8:50-9:50am

Session Overview

Picard's keynote will likely explore the fundamental question of human uniqueness in an age of increasingly sophisticated AI. As the founder of affective computing, she brings unique perspective on emotion, empathy, and the boundaries between human and machine capabilities.

Metaphor Watch: Machines vs. Humans

Expected metaphors: The "machine" metaphor for humans (Cartesian dualism), "emotional intelligence" as computational process, humans as "biological computers." Watch for how Picard navigates between mechanistic and theological anthropology.

Counter-metaphors: Humans as "image-bearers," emotions as "gifts of grace," creativity as "divine spark." These will likely challenge purely computational views of human nature.

Theological Implications

Imago Dei focus: What does it mean to be created "in God's image" when machines can simulate human emotions and responses? Picard's Christian faith will likely inform her perspective on distinctively human capacities.

Soul vs. Algorithm: Expect discussion of whether consciousness, empathy, and spiritual capacity are reducible to computational processes or represent something transcendent.

Affective Computing

Technology that recognizes and responds to human emotions - raises questions about genuine empathy vs. simulation.

Emotional Intelligence

Can machines truly understand emotions or only process emotional signals? What makes human emotional response unique?

Human-Centered AI

Designing technology that serves human flourishing rather than replacing human capacities.

Faith-Science Integration

Picard's personal journey from atheism to faith - how does Christian worldview inform AI research?

üß¨ Cultural Patterns in AI

Watch for discussion of how AI systems trained on human emotional data reflect cultural biases about emotion, gender, and appropriate emotional expression. Picard may address how affective computing systems embody certain cultural assumptions about healthy emotional life.

ü§î Expected Tensions

Reductionism vs. Emergence: Are human emotions just complex algorithms, or is there something irreducibly human about emotional experience?

Utility vs. Dignity: Should AI serve human flourishing, or does it risk instrumentalizing human emotion for technological ends?

The Frontiers of AI
Fireside Chat: Johnson, Kim, Weyl, Zhang ‚Ä¢ Thursday 7:15-9:00pm

Session Overview

This diverse panel brings together DoD's responsible AI chief, a Yale computer scientist, Microsoft's pluralistic technology researcher, and Google's DeepMind scientist. Expect wide-ranging discussion of current AI capabilities, future directions, and faith perspectives from practitioners.

Metaphor Watch: Frontiers & Exploration

"Frontier" metaphor: AI as unexplored territory, with implications of conquest, settlement, and colonization. Who gets to "explore" and "settle" these new technological territories?

"Intelligence" metaphor: What assumptions about human intelligence shape how we design and evaluate AI systems? Watch for military (Johnson), creative (Kim), economic (Weyl), and technical (Zhang) framings of intelligence.

üë• Expected Speaker Dynamics

Johnson (DoD): Responsible AI deployment, national security implications, ethical frameworks for autonomous systems.

Kim (Yale/Pixar): AI in creative industries, technical innovation in visual effects, artistic expression vs. automation.

Weyl (Microsoft): Democratic governance of AI, plurality and community decision-making, religious perspectives on technology design.

Zhang (DeepMind): Cutting-edge AI research, alignment challenges, Christians in AI community building.

Theological Implications

Stewardship vs. Dominion: How should Christians approach "frontier" AI development? Are we called to steward these technologies for common good or exercise dominion over them?

Community vs. Competition: Weyl's plurality framework vs. competitive tech development. What does Christian community look like in AI research?

üß¨ Cultural Patterns in AI Training

Zhang may discuss how AI systems absorb and amplify cultural patterns from training data. Watch for discussion of "spiritual bliss attractors" - how AI systems trained on human spiritual writing might develop particular patterns or biases toward certain spiritual frameworks.

Key Tensions to Watch

Innovation vs. Caution: Pushing AI capabilities forward vs. responsible development timelines.

Individual vs. Collective Benefit: Weyl's democratic technology vs. competitive advantage.

Military vs. Civilian Applications: Johnson's defense perspective vs. civilian AI applications.

Education in the Machine Age

Session Overview

This session explores what learning means in an age of machine intelligence. The panel combines theological anthropology (Dorobantu), AI policy (Johnson), and engineering education (Lumbreras) to examine formation, wisdom, and human development.

Metaphor Watch: Learning & Formation

"Machine Learning" vs. Human Learning: What assumptions about learning do we import from computational metaphors? How does pattern recognition differ from understanding?

Educational metaphors: Students as "data processors," knowledge as "information transfer," formation as "programming." How do these mechanistic metaphors conflict with Christian pedagogical traditions?

Counter-metaphors: Learning as "formation," knowledge as "wisdom," education as "discipleship." Watch for how speakers navigate between efficiency and transformation.

üë• Expected Speaker Dynamics

Dorobantu (Amsterdam): Theological anthropology and AI, what makes human learning distinctive, spiritual intelligence vs. artificial intelligence.

Johnson (DoD): Practical AI implementation in educational contexts, human-AI collaboration in learning environments, responsible AI in formation.

Lumbreras (Comillas): Engineering education and AI integration, Catholic perspectives on technology and formation, practical applications in higher education.

Theological Implications

Wisdom vs. Information: Biblical concepts of wisdom (sophia, phronesis) vs. information processing. What does it mean to "grow in wisdom and stature" when machines excel at information processing?

Formation vs. Training: Christian traditions emphasize character formation and spiritual development. How do AI tools support or hinder holistic human formation?

Community vs. Individualization: Learning as communal practice vs. personalized AI tutoring systems. What is lost when education becomes individualized through AI?

üß¨ Cultural Patterns in Educational AI

AI educational systems trained on particular cultural and linguistic patterns may embed specific approaches to learning, authority, and knowledge formation. Lumbreras may address how Spanish/Catholic educational traditions differ from Anglo-Protestant assumptions built into many AI systems.

Key Tensions to Watch

Efficiency vs. Formation: AI's ability to personalize and accelerate learning vs. the slow work of character formation and wisdom development.

Automation vs. Agency: AI assistance in learning vs. maintaining human intellectual and spiritual agency.

Assessment vs. Understanding: AI's capacity for assessment and measurement vs. deeper forms of understanding and wisdom.

ü§î Expected Debates

Hallucination and Truth: How do we handle AI systems that confidently present false information? What does this mean for formation in truth-seeking?

Critical Thinking: Does AI assistance enhance or atrophy human reasoning capabilities? How do we maintain intellectual virtue in AI-assisted environments?

Spiritual Formation: Can AI support spiritual formation and discipleship, or does it represent a fundamentally different approach to human development?
